## Summary
Makulukusa is a small settlement in Mashonaland West, situated about 15 km north‑west of Kadoma along the main road that links Kadoma to Harare. The landscape is a mix of open savanna and rocky outcrops of the Great Dyke, a mineral‑rich belt that supplies copper, gold and nickel. The local economy centres on mining—several small‑scale shafts and a nearby Makulukusa Mine—supplemented by mixed farming of maize, tobacco and cattle on surrounding smallholder plots. The area is served by a paved regional highway, with electricity and basic water infrastructure extending from Kadoma, making it reasonably accessible for logistics. While tourism is limited, the proximity to the Great Dyke’s geological attractions and the cultural heritage of nearby Shona villages give the region modest development potential, especially for mining‑related investment and agro‑processing ventures.
