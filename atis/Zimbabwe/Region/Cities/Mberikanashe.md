## Summary
Mberikanashe is a modest rural settlement situated in the western part of Zimbabwe’s Mashonaland West Province, roughly 70 km west of Harare along the A1 highway that links the capital with the mining town of Karoi. The surrounding landscape is a gently undulating savanna punctuated by scattered granite outcrops and the seasonal Mberikanashe River, which supports small‑scale irrigation. The community’s economy is dominated by mixed smallholder agriculture—maize, groundnuts and tobacco—supplemented by artisanal gold and chrome extraction in the nearby Great
