## Summary
Payarira is a modest rural settlement situated in the Midlands Province of Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km north‑east of the mining town of Shurugwi and accessible via a paved secondary road branching off the A5 highway. The surrounding landscape consists of gently undulating savanna‑type hills interspersed with granite outcrops and small seasonal streams that support subsistence maize, sorghum and groundnut cultivation. Small‑scale gold and chrome mining operations are scattered on the periphery, providing modest employment alongside traditional farming. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health outpost and a community market, while electricity is supplied through a nearby national grid extension. The area’s proximity to the Great Dyke mineral belt and its agricultural potential make it a focal point for modest investment in agro‑processing and mining support services.
