## Summary
Selous is a modest rural settlement in southeastern Zimbabwe’s Masvingo Province, positioned just north of the Save River and a short drive from Gonarezhou National Park. The surrounding landscape is a mix of low‑lying floodplains, mopane woodland and cultivated fields, supporting small‑scale rain‑fed maize, cotton and sorghum production as well as cattle grazing. A gravel road links Selous to the nearby town of Chiredzi, providing limited but functional access to regional markets, schools and health facilities.
