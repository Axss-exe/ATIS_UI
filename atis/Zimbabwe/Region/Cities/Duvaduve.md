**Province**: [[Manicaland]]
**Country**: [[Zimbabwe]]


## Summary
Duvaduve is a modest rural settlement in Zimbabwe’s Midlands Province, positioned a few kilometres off the A5 highway that links Harare and Bulawayo and roughly 30 km north‑west of Kwekwe. The surrounding landscape is a blend of gently rolling savanna and low‑lying granite outcrops of the Great Dyke, supporting mixed small‑holder agriculture—maize, groundnuts and cattle grazing—alongside limited artisanal mining of chrome and iron ore. The village is served by a gravel road network, a primary school, a health outpost and a weekly market that draws traders from nearby villages such as Chikomba and Gokwe. Seasonal streams provide water for irrigation
