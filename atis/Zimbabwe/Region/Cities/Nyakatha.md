## Summary
Nyakatha is a modest rural settlement in Mashonaland West, Zimbabwe, situated a few kilometres east of the A1 highway that links Harare with Bulawayo and roughly 30 km north‑west of the mining town of Kadoma. The surrounding landscape is a mix of open savanna and gently rolling farmland, with soils supporting maize, cotton and small‑scale horticulture. Proximity to the Great Dyke gives the area access to gold‑ and nickel‑bearing ore bodies, while nearby smallholder farms supply fresh produce to local markets. Infrastructure consists of a primary school, a health outpost and a gravel road network that connects Nyakatha to Kadoma and surrounding villages.
