## Summary
Chadyeni is a modest rural settlement situated in the central highlands of Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km north of the district capital of Gokwe and adjacent to the seasonal Gwayi River. The surrounding landscape consists of gently undulating savanna, interspersed with acacia woodlands and fertile alluvial soils that support small‑holder maize, sorghum and groundnut cultivation, as well as cattle grazing. A gravel‑surfaced secondary road links Chadyeni to the main A1 highway, providing limited but reliable access for traders and service vehicles; the village hosts a primary school, a health outpost, and a modest electrified market centre.
