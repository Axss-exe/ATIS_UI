## Summary
Mutasa, a district in Manicaland Province, lies in eastern Zimbabwe’s high‑relief zone, bordered by the Bvumba and Nyanga mountain ranges and the Mutare–Harare A3 highway. The landscape is a mix of rolling hills, fertile valleys and forested highlands, supporting tea, coffee, fruit orchards and small‑holder maize and tobacco farms, while granite, basalt and timber provide a modest mining and logging base. The district’s main settlements include the town of Mutasa, the nearby city of Mutare and the tourism‑focused Nyanga town, which together benefit from a paved road network, a branch railway line and limited electricity distribution. Attractions such as Nyanga National Park, Bvumba waterfalls and hot springs draw domestic visitors, and the area’s climate, water resources and proximity to Mutare make it strategically important for agricultural investment, agro‑processing and eco‑tourism development.
