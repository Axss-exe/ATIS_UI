## Summary
Nganyana is a modest rural settlement in central Zimbabwe’s Midlands Province, situated on gently rolling savanna that transitions between mopane woodland and cultivated fields. The village lies a few kilometres north of the larger town of Gokwe, which provides the nearest market, health centre and secondary school, while a single‑lane gravel road links Nganyana to the regional highway network. Residents rely chiefly on small‑holder maize, sorghum and groundnut production, supplemented by seasonal livestock grazing on communal rangeland. The area’s underlying basalt and limestone have attracted limited artisanal mining, and nearby seasonal streams support modest irrigation schemes. Although basic services such as electricity and piped water are sparse, the locality’s proximity to Gokwe and its agricultural base make it a potential focus for rural development and agribusiness investment.
