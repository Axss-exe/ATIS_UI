## Summary
Mtampane is a modest rural settlement situated in the central plateau of Zimbabwe, characterized by gently undulating savanna hills and scattered acacia woodlands. The village lies a few kilometres off the main A1 highway that links Harare to Bulawayo, with a gravel feeder road providing the primary access for trucks and passenger vehicles. The local economy revolves around small‑holder farming—predominantly maize, groundnuts and sorghum—supplemented by livestock grazing and limited artisanal mining of gold and tin along the nearby Great Dyke outcrops. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health outpost, borehole water supplies and intermittent electricity from the national grid. The area’s proximity to a seasonal wetland supports modest fishing and attracts occasional eco‑tourists interested in birdwatching and traditional cultural experiences, making Mtampane a potential focal point for modest agribusiness investment and community‑based tourism development.
