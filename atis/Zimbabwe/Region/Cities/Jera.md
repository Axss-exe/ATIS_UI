## Summary
Jera is a modest rural settlement situated on Zimbabwe’s central plateau, where gently rolling savanna grasslands give way to shallow valleys and the seasonal Mupfure River. The community lies roughly 30 km north of the town of Bindura and is linked by a paved secondary road to the A1 highway, providing relatively easy access to larger markets. Agriculture dominates the local economy, with small‑holder farms cultivating maize, tobacco and groundnuts, while cattle grazing on the surrounding communal lands supplements household incomes. A nearby limestone quarry
