## Summary
Senka is a modest settlement situated in the Midlands Province of Zimbabwe, nestled on the foothills of the Great Dyke and bordered by gently rolling savanna and cultivated fields. The locality lies roughly 30 km north of Kadoma and is linked to the regional road network via a paved secondary road that connects to the A5 highway, providing access to Harare and Bulawayo. The surrounding area supports mixed farming—maize, cotton and smallholder livestock—while nearby mineralised outcrops host artisanal gold and nickel extraction. Basic utilities such as electricity and borehole water are available, and a branch railway line passes within a few kilometres, facilitating transport of agricultural produce and ore. The combination of mineral resources, arable land and transport links makes Senka a modest yet strategically positioned hub for potential agribusiness and mining investment.
