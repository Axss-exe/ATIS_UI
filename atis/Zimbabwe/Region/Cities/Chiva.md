## Summary
Chiva is a modest rural settlement situated on the central Zimbabwean plateau, where gently undulating savanna grasslands give way to scattered acacia woodlands and low‑lying floodplains. The village lies roughly 30 km north of the district centre of Gokwe and is linked by a single‑lane gravel road that connects to the main A1 highway, providing limited but functional access for trucks and passenger vehicles. The local economy is dominated by small‑holder farming, with maize, sorghum and groundnuts cultivated on rain‑fed fields, while nearby communal lands support cattle grazing and modest cotton plots. Seasonal streams feed into the larger Zambezi basin, offering modest water resources for irrigation. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health outpost and a market that serves surrounding homesteads; electricity is supplied intermittently via a nearby diesel generator. The area’s relative isolation has kept wildlife corridors intact, attracting occasional eco‑tourism visits focused on birdwatching and guided walks, while the presence of low‑grade mineral sand deposits has drawn limited prospecting interest, making Chiva a potential focal point for targeted agricultural development and small‑scale investment in rural services.
