**Country**: [[Zimbabwe]]


## Summary
Mbizo is a high‑density residential suburb on the western edge of Kwekwe, situated on the central plateau of Zimbabwe at roughly 1,200 m elevation. The area is characterized by flat, semi‑arid terrain interspersed with low‑lying drainage channels that swell during the rainy season. It borders the Kwekwe River and lies close to the Kwekwe Mining and Smelting Company’s operations, providing many residents with informal and formal employment in mining, metal processing, and related services. Infrastructure includes paved roads linking Mbizo to the city centre, a bus terminal, primary schools, health clinics, and a growing market hub. Small‑scale horticulture and poultry farming are common in backyard plots, while nearby limestone quarries supply construction material. Although tourism is limited, the suburb’s proximity to Kwekwe’s industrial park and transport corridors makes it a focal point for community‑based development and investment in housing, retail, and vocational training.
