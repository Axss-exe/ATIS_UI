## Summary
Nhambu is a modest rural settlement situated in the central plateau of Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km north‑west of the town of Kadoma and accessible via a paved secondary road that links the A1 highway with surrounding farming communities. The landscape is characterised by gently undulating savanna grasslands interspersed with acacia woodlands and a seasonal tributary of the Zambezi basin, which supports modest irrigation. The local economy centres on small‑holder maize and cotton cultivation, cattle grazing, and limited artisanal mining of gold and nickel in nearby outcrops. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health outpost, and a community borehole, while electricity and mobile coverage are intermittent. Proximity to the historic Great Dyke mineral corridor and the potential for eco‑tourism—particularly bird‑watching along the watercourse—make Nhambu a modest yet strategically positioned site for targeted agricultural and mining investment.
