## Summary
Nyamunga is a modest rural settlement in Mashonaland West, situated about 30 km north‑east of Karoi along a gravel‑surfaced feeder road that links to the A1 highway. The surrounding landscape is characterised by gently undulating savanna, interspersed with low‑lying floodplains of the Nyamunga River, which supplies reliable surface water for small‑scale irrigation. The local economy centres on smallholder agriculture—maize, tobacco and groundnuts dominate the fields—while a few commercial farms grow cotton and horticultural crops. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health clinic, limited electricity, and a market that serves nearby villages.
