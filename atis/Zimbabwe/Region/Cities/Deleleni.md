## Summary
Deleleni is a modest rural settlement situated in Zimbabwe’s Midlands Province, positioned just off the main road linking the mining town of Kadoma to the agricultural hub of Shurugwi. The surrounding landscape is a mix of gently rolling savanna grasslands and low‑lying hills that form part of the Great Dyke’s mineral‑rich corridor. Smallholder farms dominate the local economy, producing maize, sorghum and groundnuts, while nearby artisanal mining for gold and chrome provides supplemental income. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health outpost and a community market, with electricity supplied from the national grid and a paved road granting reasonable access to regional transport routes. The area’s proximity to both mineral deposits and fertile farmland makes Deleleni a modest but strategically positioned site for agribusiness and low‑scale mining investment.
