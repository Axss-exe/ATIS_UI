## Summary
Chinzou is a rural ward in Zvimba District of Mashonaland West, situated about 45 km west of Harare along the paved A1 highway that links the capital with the northern mining towns. The landscape is a mix of gently rolling savanna and cultivated fields, with the nearby Manyame River providing irrigation for maize, tobacco and groundnut farms that dominate the local economy. Smallholder agro‑processing units and a modest market centre serve surrounding villages, while a recently upgraded electricity grid and mobile‑phone towers improve connectivity. The area’s proximity to Harare makes it a strategic node for agricultural supply chains and potential peri‑urban development, and its scenic riverbanks and community cultural sites offer modest tourism appeal.
