## Summary
Sakurai, a city in central Nara Prefecture, Japan, lies on the gently rolling Yamato Basin at the foot of the Ikoma and Kongō mountain ranges. The urban core is interspersed with historic temples such as Sakurai-ji and the renowned Sakurai Shrine, attracting cultural tourism and supporting a modest hospitality sector. The surrounding area combines residential neighborhoods with light manufacturing, particularly precision parts and electronics, while nearby agricultural land produces rice, tea, and horticultural crops. Excellent transport links include the JR West Yamatoji Line, the Kintetsu Osaka Line, and access to the Shin-Meishin Expressway, linking Sakurai to Osaka, Nara city, and the Kansai region. The city’s blend of heritage sites, convenient rail‑road connectivity, and a diversified economy makes it a strategic hub for regional development and investment.
