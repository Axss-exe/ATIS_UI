## Summary
Zival is a modest settlement situated in the central plateau of Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km north of the district capital of Gokwe and adjacent to the seasonal Zival River, which supplies irrigation for surrounding farms. The landscape is characterized by gently rolling savanna grasslands interspersed with acacia woodlands, while the nearby granite outcrops host small-scale quarrying operations that provide stone for local construction. The economy is primarily agrarian, with maize, sorghum and groundnut cultivation dominating, complemented by cattle grazing on communal rangelands. A paved secondary road links Zival to the main highway between Gokwe and Kadoma, facilitating transport of produce and access to regional markets. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health outpost, and a weekly market that draws traders from neighboring villages, while the river’s wildlife and the surrounding bush attract modest eco‑tourism and bird‑watching activities.
