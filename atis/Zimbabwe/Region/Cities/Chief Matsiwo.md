## Summary
Chief Matsiwo presides over a rural jurisdiction in northern Mashonaland Central, centred on the village of Matsiwo and extending into the surrounding savanna‑grassland plateau. The terrain is gently undulating, with loamy soils along the Mazowe River tributaries supporting mixed small‑holder farming of maize, tobacco and horticultural crops, while higher ground is used for cattle grazing. The area is accessed by a network of graded gravel roads linking Matsiwo to the district centre of Bindura, about 30 km to the southeast, and to the A1 highway. Limited electricity and mobile coverage reach the main settlement, and a modest market serves local traders. The region’s natural resources include modest timber from indigenous miombo woodlands and seasonal water points that attract modest eco‑tourism for birdwatching and cultural visits to traditional ceremonies.
