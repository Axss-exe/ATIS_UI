## Summary
Craigengower is a rural locality on the eastern fringe of Harare, centred around Craigengower Dam on the Mupfure River. The landscape consists of gently rolling high‑veld terrain, savanna grassland and scattered woodlands, with the reservoir providing a focal point for irrigation and recreation. Small farming settlements dot the area, cultivating maize, tobacco and horticultural crops, while livestock grazing is common. The dam supplies potable water to Harare’s suburbs and supports limited commercial fishing, making it a modest tourism draw for anglers and picnickers. Accessibility is good via the A2 highway linking Harare to Mutare, with a paved feeder road reaching the dam. Infrastructure
