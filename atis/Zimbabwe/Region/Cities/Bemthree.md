## Summary
Bemthree is a modest township nestled in the rolling highlands of central Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km east of the mining hub of Kadoma and 12 km north of the Shurugwi–Gwanda railway line. The surrounding landscape is characterised by gently undulating savanna grasslands interspersed with granite outcrops and scattered miombo woodland, offering a mix of arable soils and mineral‑rich substrata. The local economy centres on small‑scale gold and nickel extraction from the nearby Great Dyke, complemented by subsistence maize and cotton farming that benefits from seasonal rainfall and irrigation schemes fed by the Upper Manyame River. A paved secondary road links Bemthree to the A5 highway, providing access to larger markets, while a modest service centre supplies fuel, basic retail, and a health clinic. Recent investment interest has focused on expanding the mining concession and developing eco‑tourism lodges that showcase the region’s geological formations and bird‑watching opportunities, positioning Bemthree as a strategic node for both resource extraction and sustainable tourism development.
