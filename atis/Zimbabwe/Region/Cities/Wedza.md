## Summary
Wedza, a town in Mashonaland East, sits amid rolling granite hills and fertile valleys at about 1,200 m elevation, giving it a temperate climate suited to mixed farming. The surrounding district produces tobacco, maize, beans and horticultural crops, while small‑scale gold, nickel and granite mining add to the local economy. Road links, notably the A2 highway and secondary routes to Marondera and Murehwa, provide reasonable access, though rail service is limited. The area benefits from the Mupfure River and several springs that support irrigation and livestock. Scenic highlands, wildlife‑rich woodlands and cultural sites attract modest tourism, and the region’s agricultural base, mineral resources, and improving infrastructure make it a focal point for agro‑processing and mining investment.
