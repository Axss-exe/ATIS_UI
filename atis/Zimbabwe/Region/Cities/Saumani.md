## Summary
Saumani is a modest rural settlement in Gokwe South District of Midlands Province, situated on gently rolling, semi‑arid savanna that transitions to low‑lying floodplains near the seasonal Gokwe River. The village is linked to the district centre by a single‑track gravel road, providing limited but functional access for trucks and minibuses. Residents rely chiefly on small‑holder agriculture—maize, sorghum and groundnuts—supplemented by cattle and goat grazing on communal rangeland. A modest market centre serves surrounding farms, while a primary school and a health outpost constitute the core public infrastructure.
