## Summary
Mtoredzanwe is a modest rural settlement situated in the Midlands Province of Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km north‑east of the district centre of Gokwe and adjacent to the eastern fringe of the Great Dyke geological formation. The surrounding landscape consists of gently undulating savanna grassland interspersed with acacia woodlands and low‑lying riverine wetlands fed by seasonal streams. Residents primarily engage in small‑holder agriculture—maize, groundnuts and tobacco—supplemented by livestock grazing and limited artisanal mining of chrome and nickel associated with the Dyke. The village is linked to the regional road network by an unpaved, all‑weather track, with electricity supplied from a nearby mini‑grid and water sourced from communal boreholes.
