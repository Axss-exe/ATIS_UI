**Country**: [[Zimbabwe]]


## Summary
Wenzera is a modest settlement in Manicaland Province, situated on the A2 highway and the main railway line that links Mutare with the Mozambique border, making it a key transit point for cross‑border trade. The town lies amid gently rolling hills and fertile valleys, with the Save River basin providing irrigation for surrounding farms that grow sugarcane, tea, coffee and maize, while nearby forested slopes support timber extraction. Infrastructure includes a border post, a small market, primary schools and a health centre, with road access to Mutare (≈30 km) and an airstrip at Mutare Airport. The area’s natural scenery, including the Bvumba Mountains, attracts limited eco‑tourism and bird‑watching, and its agricultural potential and strategic location have drawn interest for agro‑processing and logistics investment.
