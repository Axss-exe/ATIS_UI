## Summary
Danda is a modest rural settlement situated in the Midlands Province of Zimbabwe, lying on the lower reaches of the Danda River and a few kilometres off the A1 highway that links Harare to Bulawayo. The surrounding landscape is characterised by gently rolling savanna, scattered miombo woodland and seasonal floodplains that support smallholder maize, sorghum and groundnut farms, as well as extensive cattle grazing. The local economy is supplemented by informal gold‑bearing quartz mining and a modest timber trade from nearby woodlots. Infrastructure includes a primary school, a health clinic, a market centre and a gravel‑surfaced feeder road that connects Danda to the district capital of Gokwe.
