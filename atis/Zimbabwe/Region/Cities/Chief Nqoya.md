## Summary
Chief Nqoya presides over a rural catch‑area in the central Midlands of Zimbabwe, centred on the rolling savanna‑grassland of the Zhombe Valley and bounded by the Mupfure River to the east. The chiefdom’s settlements cluster around the small town of Silobela, with satellite villages linked by a network of tarred and gravel roads that connect to the A5 highway and the nearby mining hub of Kadoma. The local economy is a mix of small‑holder maize, cotton and tobacco farms, livestock grazing, and artisanal gold mining on the surrounding quartz veins. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health clinic, and a community centre, while the river provides limited irrigation and fishing opportunities. Seasonal wildlife and the proximity to the historic Great Dyke make the area modestly attractive for eco‑tourism and potential agribusiness investment.
