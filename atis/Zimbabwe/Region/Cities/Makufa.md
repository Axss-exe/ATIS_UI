## Summary
Makufa is a modest rural settlement in Zimbabwe’s Midlands Province, positioned on the low‑lying floodplain of the Makufu River, a tributary of the Zambezi that supplies seasonal irrigation and supports a mixed farming economy. The surrounding landscape consists of open savanna woodland interspersed with cultivated fields of maize, sorghum and small‑scale cotton plots,
