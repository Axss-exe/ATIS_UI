## Summary
Tshayakuhle is a modest rural settlement situated in the central highlands of Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km north of the district capital Gokwe and adjacent to the seasonal Gwayi River. The surrounding landscape is characterised by gently rolling savanna, scattered granite outcrops and fertile alluvial soils that support mixed farming of maize, sorghum and cattle grazing. Smallholder agriculture dominates the local economy, while nearby limestone and gold deposits have attracted limited artisanal mining activities. The village is linked to the regional road network by a paved secondary road, providing access to larger markets in Gokwe and the provincial hub of Kadoma. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health outpost and a community centre, and recent upgrades to electricity distribution have improved reliability. The area’s proximity to wildlife‑rich woodlands and the river offers modest eco‑tourism potential, making Tshayakuhle a focal point for development initiatives aimed at enhancing agricultural productivity, value‑added processing and sustainable resource management.
