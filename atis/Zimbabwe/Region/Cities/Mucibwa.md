## Summary
Mucibwa is a modest rural settlement in Mashonaland West Province, situated roughly 70 km west of Harare and linked to the main Harare‑Bulawayo highway by a secondary road. The surrounding landscape is a blend of gently rolling savanna and cultivated fields, with smallholder farms producing maize, tobacco and groundnuts, while nearby outcrops of the Great Dyke provide limited artisanal mining of gold and chrome. Infrastructure is basic: a primary school, a health outpost and a few retail kiosks serve the community, and electricity reaches only part of the village. The area’s water supply relies on shallow boreholes and seasonal streams.
