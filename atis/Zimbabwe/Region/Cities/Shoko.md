## Summary
Shoko is a modest settlement situated on Zimbabwe’s central plateau, roughly 120 km north of Harare in Mashonaland Central. The town lies amid gently rolling savanna, with fertile loam supporting maize, tobacco and small‑holder horticulture, while nearby livestock grazing is common. It is flanked by the larger towns of Murehwa and Bindura, and the A1 highway provides paved access to the capital and the northern mining districts. Electricity, a primary school, a health clinic and a weekly market serve the community, and a seasonal tributary of the Shoko River supplies
