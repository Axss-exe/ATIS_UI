## Summary
Nazvaure is a modest rural settlement in Zimbabwe’s Midlands Province, situated on gently rolling savanna that transitions into low‑lying granite outcrops. The village lies roughly 30 km north of Kadoma and is linked to the regional A1 highway by a paved feeder road, providing reasonable access to Harare and the copper‑mining belt. Agriculture dominates the local economy, with smallholder farms cultivating maize, groundnuts and tobacco, while nearby communal lands support cattle grazing. The area’s sub‑soil contains modest gold and nickel deposits, prompting limited artisanal mining activity. Basic services include a primary school, a health outpost and a weekly market, while the surrounding bushveld offers modest eco‑tourism potential for birdwatching and hiking.
