## Summary
Kotwa is a modest border town in Mudzi District, Mashonaland Central, situated on the eastern edge of the Zambezi Valley near the Zimbabwe‑Mozambique frontier. The surrounding landscape is a low‑lying savanna punctuated by riverine wetlands, offering fertile soils for small‑holder crops such as maize, cotton and tobacco, and supporting livestock grazing. The town serves as a key customs post on the A2 highway, linking Harare with the port city of Beira, and its road and limited electricity infrastructure facilitate cross‑border trade, informal commerce, and regional transport. Proximity to the Zambezi River provides water for irrigation, fishing opportunities, and nascent tourism potential through river cruises and wildlife viewing, making Kotwa strategically important for trade, agricultural development, and investment in transport corridors.
