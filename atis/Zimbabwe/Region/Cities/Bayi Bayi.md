## Summary
Bayi Bayi is a modest rural settlement situated on the gently rolling plateau of north‑central Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km east of the market town of Karoi and a short distance south of the A1 highway that links Harare with Chinhoyi. The surrounding landscape is a mix of open savanna grassland and cultivated fields, with fertile loam supporting maize, cotton and small‑holder horticulture, while nearby seasonal streams feed modest irrigation schemes. The village is linked by a single‑lane gravel road, providing limited but reliable access for trucks transporting agricultural produce to regional collection points. Economic activity centres on farming, a few artisanal gold‑bearing gravels, and a seasonal livestock market; basic services include a primary school,
