## Summary
Dabula is a modest settlement on Zimbabwe’s central plateau, roughly 30 km north of Gweru in Midlands Province. The landscape consists of gently rolling savanna grasslands dotted with acacia woodlands, while the nearby Munyati River supplies water for irrigation and livestock. The local economy centres on small‑holder maize and tobacco farms, cattle grazing, and a modest granite quarry that provides stone for regional construction projects. A paved secondary road connects Dabula to the A1 highway, affording access to Gweru and Harare, and the village is served by electricity, a basic health clinic, and a primary school.
