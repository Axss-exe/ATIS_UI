## Summary
Pambuka is a modest rural settlement situated in the eastern part of Zimbabwe’s Mashonaland East Province, roughly 30 km north‑east of the district centre of Murewa and a short drive off the A2 highway that links Harare with the eastern border. The surrounding landscape is a patchwork of gently rolling savanna, cultivated fields and scattered miombo woodland, with the nearby Nyaminyami River providing limited irrigation. The local economy centres on small‑holder maize, groundnut and cotton farming, complemented by seasonal labour in nearby gold‑bearing outcrops of the Great Dyke.
