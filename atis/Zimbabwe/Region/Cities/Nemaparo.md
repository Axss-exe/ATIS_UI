## Summary
Nemaparo is a rural ward in Zvimba District, Mashonaland West Province, situated on the undulating savanna that slopes gently toward the Upper Zambezi basin. The settlement lies about 45 km west of Harare, with the nearest town, Murombedzi, 12 km to the south, providing access to a modest market and primary health centre. Residents rely chiefly on small‑holder maize, groundnut and cotton cultivation, supplemented by livestock grazing on communal rangeland. A paved secondary road links Nemaparo to the A1 highway, facilitating transport of produce to regional traders, while a recent electrification project has extended power to several homesteads. The area’s modest tourism appeal stems from nearby wildlife‑friendly woodlands and seasonal birding hotspots, making it a potential site for agro‑processing investment and community‑based ecotourism initiatives.
