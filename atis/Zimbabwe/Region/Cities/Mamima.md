## Summary
Mamima is a modest rural settlement situated in the Midlands Province of Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km north‑west of the mining town of Kadoma and adjacent to the A1 highway that links Harare with Bulawayo. The surrounding landscape is a mix of gently rolling savanna and cultivated fields, with fertile soils supporting maize, tobacco and small‑scale horticulture, while nearby outcrops of the Great Dyke provide limited gold and chrome extraction. The community is served by a primary school, a health clinic and a market centre, and electricity is supplied via the national grid, though water is sourced from boreholes. Road access is decent, with a paved link to Kadoma and a secondary gravel road reaching neighboring villages. Mamima’s proximity to mining activity and its agricultural base make it a modest but strategically positioned node for agro‑processing and logistics investment.
