## Summary
Nyandebvu is a rural settlement in Mashonaland West, situated roughly 30 km west of Harare along the A1 highway corridor. The landscape is characterised by gently undulating savanna interspersed with granite outcrops and the Nyandebvu River, which provides seasonal water for irrigation and livestock. The local economy centres on mixed small‑holder agriculture—maize, groundnuts and tobacco—supplemented by artisanal mining of gold and other minerals associated with the nearby Great Dyke belt. The village is connected to the national electricity grid and benefits from a paved road network that links it to Harare and surrounding towns such as Kadoma. Borehole schemes supply potable water, while a modest market serves traders from adjacent villages. Its proximity to major transport routes and mineral resources makes Nyandebvu a modest but strategically positioned hub for agricultural development and potential mining investment.
