## Summary
Nyadzi is a modest rural settlement in Mashonaland East, positioned on the banks of the Nyadzi River about 70 km east of Harare and a few kilometres off the A2 highway that links the capital with Mutare. The surrounding landscape is a mix of gently rolling savanna and cultivated fields, with maize, tobacco and small‑holder cattle grazing dominating the agricultural output. A primary school, a health clinic and a recently upgraded gravel road provide basic infrastructure, while electricity is supplied from the national grid and boreholes serve most households.
