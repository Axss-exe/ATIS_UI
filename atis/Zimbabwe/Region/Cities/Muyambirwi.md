## Summary
Muyambirwi is a modest rural settlement in northern Zimbabwe, situated on the gently rolling savanna of Mashonaland Central near the confluence of the Manyame and Zambezi river basins. The surrounding landscape is characterized by open grasslands punctuated by acacia woodlands and small-scale irrigation schemes that support maize, millet and tobacco cultivation, while nearby cattle ranches exploit the natural grazing. The village lies a few kilometres off the A1 highway, providing reasonable road access to the provincial capital of Bindura and larger markets in Harare.
