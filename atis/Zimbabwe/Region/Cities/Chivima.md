## Summary
Chivima is a modest rural settlement in north‑western Zimbabwe, situated on the eastern bank of the Zambezi River near the border with Zambia. The surrounding landscape is a mix of low‑lying floodplains, riverine wetlands and gently rolling savanna, supporting small‑scale subsistence farming of maize, millet and groundnuts, as well as commercial cotton and tobacco on the drier uplands. A paved secondary road links Chivima to the district centre of Binga and to the main highway that runs along the Zambezi, providing limited but reliable access for trucks
