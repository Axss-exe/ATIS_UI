## Summary
Nembudzia is a modest rural settlement situated in the central Midlands of Zimbabwe, roughly 30 km north‑east of the district capital of Gokwe and adjacent to the Zambezi‑bound Ngondoma River. The surrounding landscape is a mix of gently rolling savanna, scattered miombo woodland and cultivated fields, with the river’s floodplain supporting seasonal wetlands. Smallholder farms dominate the economy, producing maize, sorghum and ground
