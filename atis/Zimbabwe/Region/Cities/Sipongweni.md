## Summary
Sipongweni is a small, semi‑arid settlement in Matabeleland South Province, roughly 30 km north‑east of Gwanda and situated along the A6 highway that links Bulawayo with the Beitbridge border post. The landscape consists of open savanna, scattered granite outcrops and low‑lying riverbeds that support limited rain‑fed agriculture—mainly sorghum, millet and groundnuts—while cattle grazing dominates the surrounding rangelands. The village is served by a primary school, a health clinic, borehole water supplies and intermittent electricity from the national grid; a paved road provides easy access to regional markets.
