## Summary
Mvurwi is a modest town in Mashonaland Central, situated about 100 km north of Harare along the A1 highway and a branch railway that links it to the capital and the mining hub of Bindura. The surrounding high‑veld landscape is characterised by gently rolling hills, savanna grassland and the Mvurwi River, which feeds a small dam used for irrigation. The local economy centres on mineral extraction—particularly copper and gold at the Mvurwi Mine—and on commercial agriculture, with tobacco, maize and horticultural crops cultivated on the fertile soils. Infrastructure includes a district hospital, secondary schools, a market centre and basic utilities, while road and rail connections provide reasonable access for trade. Though tourism is limited, the area’s scenic vistas and bird‑watching opportunities add modest appeal, and its proximity to Harare and resource base make it a focal point for investment in mining and agro‑processing.
