## Summary
Gokwe, a town in the north‑western part of Midlands Province, sits on a gently undulating savanna landscape marked by low hills, scattered granite outcrops and the seasonal Gokwe River. It serves as the commercial hub for Gokwe North and South districts, linking nearby settlements such as Shurugwi, Kadoma and the larger A1 highway corridor that connects Harare to Bulawayo. The local economy is dominated by mixed farming—maize, cotton, tobacco and cattle ranching—supplemented by small‑scale mining of gold, nickel and limestone, and a growing agro‑processing sector. Infrastructure includes paved roads, a modest airstrip, electricity distribution, borehole water schemes and a district hospital. The area’s natural resources, relatively low land costs and position as a gateway to north‑western Zimbabwe make it attractive for investment in agriculture, mining and renewable‑energy projects, while nearby wildlife reserves and cultural sites offer limited tourism potential.
