## Summary
Mbizo is a high‑density township on the southern edge of Kwekwe in Zimbabwe’s Midlands Province, set on a flat, semi‑arid savanna landscape near the Sebakwe River. It is bounded by the Kwekwe‑Gokwe highway and a railway line that link the city to regional mining and manufacturing centres, giving the area good road and rail accessibility. The local economy is dominated by informal trade, small‑scale manufacturing, and peri‑urban agriculture, with residents cultivating maize, sorghum and vegetables on modest plots. Basic infrastructure includes electricity, water reticulation, primary schools and a health centre, though services are often strained by rapid population growth. Proximity to Kwekwe’s coal, gold and limestone deposits, as well as to the Sebakwe Dam and museum, makes Mbizo strategically important for housing, labour supply and potential investment in urban services and light industry.
