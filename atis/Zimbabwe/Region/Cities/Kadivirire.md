## Summary
Kadovirire is a modest rural settlement in Mashonaland West Province, situated roughly 30 km north‑east of the district centre of Karoi and accessed via a paved secondary road that links it to the A1 highway. The surrounding landscape is characterised by gently rolling savanna, interspersed with low‑lying riverine flats along the nearby Mukamba River, which support seasonal irrigation. The local economy centres on small‑holder agriculture—predominantly maize, groundnuts and tobacco—supplemented by limited livestock rearing and a modest informal mining sector for gold and nickel. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health outpost and a community market, while electricity is supplied through a regional grid extension. Proximity to Karoi provides residents with access to larger commercial services, and the area’s natural scenery and bird‑watching potential have begun to attract low‑key eco‑tourism initiatives, making Kadovirire a focal point for modest investment in agricultural value‑addition and rural development.
