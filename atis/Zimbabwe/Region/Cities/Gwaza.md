## Summary
Gwaza is a modest rural settlement in Mashonaland West, situated a few kilometres east of Kadoma and linked to the A1 highway by a paved secondary road. The surrounding landscape is a gently undulating savanna dotted with miombo woodland and smallholder farms that grow maize, sorghum and groundnuts, while nearby cattle grazing paddocks benefit from seasonal streams feeding the Gwaza River. The area’s economy is anchored in agriculture, with a growing interest in agro‑processing and limited artisanal gold mining that taps into the region’s mineralised belts. Basic infrastructure includes a primary school, a health outpost and electricity supplied to the village centre, while a bus service provides regular access to Kadoma’s markets and services. The locality’s proximity to transport corridors and its blend of farming and mining potential make it a modest yet strategically positioned node for rural development and investment.
