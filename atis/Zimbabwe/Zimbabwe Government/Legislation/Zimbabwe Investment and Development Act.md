# Zimbabwe Investment and Development Act

**Status**: Active
**Enforced By**: 
  - "[[Zimbabwe Investment and Development Agency (ZIDA)]]"


## Description
The ZIDA Act is the cornerstone of Zimbabwe's foreign direct investment (FDI) framework, enacted in 2019. It established ZIDA to act as a "One-Stop Investment Services Centre" (OSISC), designed to streamline bureaucratic bottlenecks by housing key agencies (like tax, immigration, and environmental management) under one roof. The Act consolidated and repealed three major previous laws: the Zimbabwe Investment Authority Act, the Joint Ventures Act, and the Special Economic Zones Act. For foreign investors, its most critical provisions are the legal guarantees against state expropriation of assets, the protected rights to repatriate profits and dividends, and the established frameworks for international dispute resolution.
