## Mining Lease / Mining Title

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Ministry of Mines and Mining Development]]
**Legislation**: [[Mines and Minerals Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: Prospecting Rights
**Processing Time**: 90 to 180 working days


## Description
The **Ministry of Mines and Mining Development** issues a Mining Lease, also known as a Mining Title, under the provisions of the **[[Mines and Minerals Act]]**, granting the holder exclusive rights to explore, develop, and extract specified mineral resources within a defined geographic area; the lease delineates the scope of permissible activities, obligates the lessee to adhere to environmental and safety standards, mandates the payment of royalties and fees, and includes provisions for renewal, suspension, or cancellation should the holder fail to meet statutory requirements or the terms of the agreement.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Identify ground"
  - "2. Appoint Approved Prospector"
  - "3. Pegging and posting of notices"
  - "4. Submit application to Provincial Mining Director"
  - "5. Review by Mining Affairs Board"
  - "6. Issuance of title"

## Required Documents
  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Coordinates/Map"
  - "Prospecting License"
  - "Pegger's Certificate"
  - "Payment of pegging fees"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Disputes over coordinates (overpegging)"
  - "Mining Affairs Board meeting schedules"
  - "Manual cadastral system issues"
