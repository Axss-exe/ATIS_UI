## Data Service Provider Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)]]
**Legislation**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Act]] 
**Prerequisite Permits**: Company Registration
**Processing Time**: 60 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Data Service Provider Licence** is a regulatory authorization granted by the **Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)** under the provisions of the **[[Postal and Telecommunications Act]] [Chapter 12:05]**, which permits entities to offer data transmission, internet access, and related digital communication services within Zimbabwe; the licence obliges holders to comply with technical standards, consumer protection rules, and spectrum management policies, while also ensuring that their operations contribute to the development of the national telecommunications infrastructure and adhere to the authority’s licensing conditions and reporting requirements.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Detailed technical submission"
  - "2. Potraz review"
  - "3. Security clearance"
  - "4. Gazetting"
  - "5. License award"

  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Network Topology"
  - "Vendor Contracts"
  - "Frequency Allocation Request"
  - "Security Clearance Forms"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Spectrum unavailability"
  - "Intense security vetting"
  - "High licensing fees"
