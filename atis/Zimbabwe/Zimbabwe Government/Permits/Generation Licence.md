## Generation Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA)]]
**Legislation**: [[Electricity Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: EIA Certificate
**Processing Time**: 90 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Generation Licence** issued by the **Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA)** under the [[Electricity Act]] is a regulatory instrument that authorises an entity to develop, construct, operate, and maintain electricity‑generating facilities within Zimbabwe, ensuring that the proposed project meets technical, environmental, and financial standards; it obliges the holder to comply with grid‑connection requirements, adhere to prescribed safety and performance criteria, and submit regular reports on output and compliance, while also granting ZERA the authority to monitor, enforce conditions, and, if necessary, suspend or revoke the licence to protect the reliability and sustainability of the national power system.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Submit technical and financial proposal"
  - "2. ZERA initial review"
  - "3. Public notice/gazetting (14 days)"
  - "4. Technical compliance check"
  - "5. Board approval"
  - "6. License issuance"

## Required Documents
  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Grid Impact Study"
  - "Proof of Land Rights"
  - "PPA (if IPP)"
  - "Financial Capacity Proof"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Public objection resolution"
  - "Grid capacity limitations"
  - "PPA negotiation delays"
