## Courier Operator Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)]]
**Legislation**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: Company Registration
**Processing Time**: 60 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Courier Operator Licence** issued by the **Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)** under the [[Postal and Telecommunications Act]] Chapter 12:05 authorises entities to provide courier and parcel‑delivery services across the country, ensuring that operators meet stringent standards for security, reliability, and consumer protection; the licence mandates compliance with regulated pricing structures, tracking and reporting requirements, and adherence to national and international postal conventions, thereby fostering a trustworthy and accountable logistics sector that supports both commercial and personal communications while safeguarding the integrity of the national postal network.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Detailed technical submission"
  - "2. Potraz review"
  - "3. Security clearance"
  - "4. Gazetting"
  - "5. License award"

## Required Documents
  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Network Topology"
  - "Vendor Contracts"
  - "Frequency Allocation Request"
  - "Security Clearance Forms"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Spectrum unavailability"
  - "Intense security vetting"
  - "High licensing fees"
