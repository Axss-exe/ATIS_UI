## Internet Access Provider (IAP) Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)]]
**Legislation**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Act]] 
**Prerequisite Permits**: Company Registration
**Processing Time**: 60 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Internet Access Provider (IAP) Licence** is a regulatory instrument granted by the **Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)** under the provisions of the [[Postal and Telecommunications Act ]] [Chapter 12:05]; it authorises entities to offer internet connectivity services to the public, stipulates compliance with technical standards, consumer protection measures, and spectrum usage rules, and requires licence holders to maintain service quality, report operational data, and adhere to licensing fees and renewal conditions, thereby ensuring a structured and accountable framework for the development of Zimbabwe’s broadband ecosystem.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Detailed technical submission"
  - "2. Potraz review"
  - "3. Security clearance"
  - "4. Gazetting"
  - "5. License award"

## Required Documents
  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Network Topology"
  - "Vendor Contracts"
  - "Frequency Allocation Request"
  - "Security Clearance Forms"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Spectrum unavailability"
  - "Intense security vetting"
  - "High licensing fees"
