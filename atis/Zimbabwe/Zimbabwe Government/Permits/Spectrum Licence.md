## Spectrum Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)]]
**Legislation**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: Telecommunications Licence
**Processing Time**: 60 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Spectrum Licence** issued by the **Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)** under the **[[Postal and Telecommunications Act]]** is a regulatory instrument that authorises the allocation and use of specific radio frequency bands to telecommunications operators, broadcasters, and other service providers within Zimbabwe; it sets out the technical parameters, duration, fees, and compliance obligations required to ensure efficient spectrum management, prevent interference, and promote fair competition while supporting the country’s broader objectives of expanding connectivity, fostering innovation, and safeguarding national security.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Detailed technical submission"
  - "2. Potraz review"
  - "3. Security clearance"
  - "4. Gazetting"
  - "5. License award"

  - "Certificate of Incorporation"

  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Network Topology"
  - "Vendor Contracts"
  - "Frequency Allocation Request"
  - "Security Clearance Forms"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Spectrum unavailability"
  - "Intense security vetting"
  - "High licensing fees"
