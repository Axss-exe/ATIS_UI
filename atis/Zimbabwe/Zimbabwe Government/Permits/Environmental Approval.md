## Environmental Approval

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Environmental Management Agency (EMA)]]
**Legislation**: [[Environmental Management Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: EIA Certificate
**Processing Time**: 60 working days


## Description
The **Environmental Management Agency (EMA)** issues an Environmental Approval under the **[[Environmental Management Act]]** to formally certify that a proposed project or activity complies with all applicable environmental standards, regulations, and mitigation requirements; this approval confirms that the applicant has conducted thorough assessments, incorporated necessary safeguards to protect ecosystems, and secured any required conditions or monitoring plans, thereby granting legal permission to proceed while ensuring that environmental impacts are minimized and managed in accordance with national policy objectives.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Submit Prospectus"
  - "2. EMA approves terms of reference"
  - "3. Conduct EIA study"
  - "4. Submit EIA Report"
  - "5. Public consultation review"
  - "6. EMA site verification"
  - "7. Issue certificate"

## Required Documents
  - "EIA Prospectus"
  - "Full EIA Report"
  - "Proof of Public Consultation"
  - "Consultant Certification"
  - "Project Maps"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Inadequate public consultation evidence"
  - "Rejection of Prospectus"
  - "EMA capacity limits for site visits"
