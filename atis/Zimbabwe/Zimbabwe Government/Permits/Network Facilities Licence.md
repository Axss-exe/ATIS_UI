## Network Facilities Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)]]
**Legislation**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: EIA where required
**Processing Time**: 60 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)** issues a Network Facilities Licence under the [[Postal and Telecommunications Act]] to authorize the construction, operation, and maintenance of telecommunications infrastructure such as fiber‑optic cables, microwave links, and related transmission equipment; the licence ensures that operators meet technical standards, adhere to spectrum management rules, and comply with service quality, safety, and security obligations, while also providing a framework for the allocation of rights‑of‑way, interconnection agreements, and the protection of consumer interests, thereby fostering a competitive and reliable communications environment across Zimbabwe.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Detailed technical submission"
  - "2. Potraz review"
  - "3. Security clearance"
  - "4. Gazetting"
  - "5. License award"

## Required Documents
  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Network Topology"
  - "Vendor Contracts"
  - "Frequency Allocation Request"
  - "Security Clearance Forms"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Spectrum unavailability"
  - "Intense security vetting"
  - "High licensing fees"
