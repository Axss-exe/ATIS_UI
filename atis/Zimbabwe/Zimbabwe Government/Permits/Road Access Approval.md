## Road Access Approval

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Zimbabwe National Road Administration (ZINARA)]]; Local Authority
**Legislation**: Roads Legislation
**Prerequisite Permits**: EIA Certificate
**Processing Time**: 14 to 30 working days


## Description
The **Road Access Approval** is a formal permit issued by **Zimbabwe National Road Administration (ZINARA)**, acting as the **Local Authority** under the Roads Legislation, to authorize the construction, modification, or use of any structure, service line, or development that intersects or abuts a public road or right‑of‑way; it ensures that all works comply with national safety standards, traffic management requirements, and engineering specifications, and it obliges applicants to submit detailed plans, obtain necessary clearances, and coordinate with relevant stakeholders before any ground‑disturbing activities commence, thereby safeguarding the integrity of the road network and minimizing disruption to road users.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Prepare proposal"
  - "2. Complete application"
  - "3. Submit documents"
  - "4. Pay application fees"
  - "5. Technical review/Due diligence"
  - "6. Site inspection (if applicable)"
  - "7. Board/Committee approval"
  - "8. Receive licence"

## Required Documents
  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Incomplete applications"
  - "Delays in statutory board meetings"
  - "Slow cross-agency verification"
  - "Site inspection delays"
