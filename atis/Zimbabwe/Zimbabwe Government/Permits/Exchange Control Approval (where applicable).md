## Exchange Control Approval (where applicable)

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe (RBZ)]]
**Legislation**: Exchange Control Regulations
**Prerequisite Permits**: Investment Approval
**Processing Time**: 14 to 30 working days


## Description
The **Exchange Control Approval** is a formal authorization issued by the **Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe (RBZ)** under the provisions of the **Exchange Control Regulations**, required whenever a transaction involving foreign exchange falls outside the routine exemptions or exceeds prescribed limits; the approval confirms that the proposed inflow or outflow complies with the country’s foreign‑exchange policy, validates the purpose of the transaction, and ensures that the necessary documentation and supporting evidence have been reviewed and deemed satisfactory, thereby allowing the applicant to proceed with the cross‑border payment or receipt in accordance with the RBZ’s regulatory framework.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Lodge application via Authorised Dealer (Commercial Bank)"
  - "2. Bank submits to RBZ Exchange Control"
  - "3. Review by RBZ"
  - "4. Issuance of Exchange Control Authority"

## Required Documents
  - "Application via Bank"
  - "Invoices/Agreements"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Acquittal records for previous exports"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Strict forex regulations"
  - "Request for further documentation"
  - "Foreign currency availability"
