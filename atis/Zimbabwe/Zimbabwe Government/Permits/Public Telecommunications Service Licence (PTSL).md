## Public Telecommunications Service Licence (PTSL)

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)]]
**Legislation**: [[[Postal and Telecommunications Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: Company Registration
**Processing Time**: 60 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Public Telecommunications Service Licence (PTSL)** is a regulatory instrument granted by the **Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)** under the provisions of the [[Postal and Telecommunications Act]]  [Chapter 12:05]; it authorises entities to operate public telecommunications networks and provide services such as voice, data, and internet access to the general public, subject to compliance with technical standards, quality of service obligations, and financial and reporting requirements designed to promote competition, protect consumer interests, and ensure the sustainable development of Zimbabwe’s telecommunications infrastructure.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Detailed technical submission"
  - "2. Potraz review"
  - "3. Security clearance"
  - "4. Gazetting"
  - "5. License award"

## Required Documents
  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Network Topology"
  - "Vendor Contracts"
  - "Frequency Allocation Request"
  - "Security Clearance Forms"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Spectrum unavailability"
  - "Intense security vetting"
  - "High licensing fees"
