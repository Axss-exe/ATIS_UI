## Radio Station Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)]]
**Legislation**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: Spectrum Assignment
**Processing Time**: 60 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)** issues a Radio Station Licence pursuant to the **[[[Postal and Telecommunications Act]] [Chapter 12:05]**, which authorises broadcasters to operate legally within the country’s spectrum framework; the licence sets out the technical parameters, content standards, and compliance obligations that applicants must meet, including the allocation of frequencies, adherence to national broadcasting codes, payment of applicable fees, and ongoing reporting requirements, thereby ensuring that radio services are delivered in a manner that promotes fair competition, protects public interest, and aligns with Zimbabwe’s regulatory policies for telecommunications and media.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Detailed technical submission"
  - "2. Potraz review"
  - "3. Security clearance"
  - "4. Gazetting"
  - "5. License award"
