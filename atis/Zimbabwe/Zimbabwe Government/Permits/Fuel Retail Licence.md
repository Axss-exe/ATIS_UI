## Fuel Retail Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA)]]
**Legislation**: [[Petroleum Regulations]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: Fuel Storage Approval
**Processing Time**: 90 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Fuel Retail Licence** is a regulatory permit granted by the **Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA)** under the Petroleum Regulations, authorising entities to engage in the wholesale or retail sale of petroleum products such as gasoline, diesel, and kerosene within Zimbabwe’s borders; the licence requires applicants to demonstrate compliance with safety, environmental, and financial standards, maintain adequate storage and dispensing infrastructure, and adhere to pricing and reporting obligations, thereby ensuring that fuel is supplied responsibly, competitively, and in line with national energy policy objectives.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Submit technical and financial proposal"
  - "2. ZERA initial review"
  - "3. Public notice/gazetting (14 days)"
  - "4. Technical compliance check"
  - "5. Board approval"
  - "6. License issuance"

## Required Documents
  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Grid Impact Study"
  - "Proof of Land Rights"
  - "PPA (if IPP)"
  - "Financial Capacity Proof"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Public objection resolution"
  - "Grid capacity limitations"
  - "PPA negotiation delays"
