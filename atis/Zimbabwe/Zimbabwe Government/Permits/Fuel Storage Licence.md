## Fuel Storage Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA)]]
**Legislation**: [[Mineral and Petroleum Resources Development Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: EIA Certificate
**Processing Time**: 90 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Fuel Storage Licence** is a regulatory permit granted by the **Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA)** under the **[[Mineral and Petroleum Resources Development Act]]** framework, authorising entities to construct, operate, and maintain facilities for the safe storage of petroleum products such as gasoline, diesel, kerosene and lubricants. The licence ensures that storage installations meet stringent technical, environmental and safety standards, including fire‑prevention measures, spill containment, and regular inspections, thereby protecting public health, the environment and the integrity of the national fuel supply chain. Holders must comply with reporting obligations, maintain up‑to‑date operational records, and undergo periodic audits to retain the licence, which can be revoked or suspended for non‑compliance or safety breaches.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Submit technical and financial proposal"
  - "2. ZERA initial review"
  - "3. Public notice/gazetting (14 days)"
  - "4. Technical compliance check"
  - "5. Board approval"
  - "6. License issuance"

  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Grid Impact Study"
  - "Proof of Land Rights"
  - "PPA (if IPP)"
  - "Financial Capacity Proof"
