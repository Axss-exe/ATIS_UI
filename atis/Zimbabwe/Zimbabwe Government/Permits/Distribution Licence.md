## Distribution Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA)]]
**Legislation**: [[Electricity Act]]
**Prerequisite Permits**: Generation Licence (if applicable)
**Processing Time**: 90 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Distribution Licence** issued by **Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA)** under the **[[Electricity Act]]** is a regulatory instrument that authorises an entity to own, operate, maintain and develop the electricity distribution network within a defined service area, ensuring that the infrastructure meets safety, reliability and quality standards; it obliges the licensee to adhere to tariff frameworks, performance benchmarks and consumer protection provisions set by the Authority, while also requiring compliance with technical specifications, reporting duties and investment commitments aimed at expanding access and enhancing the efficiency of the power supply system across Zimbabwe.


## Standard Operating Procedure
  - "1. Submit technical and financial proposal"
  - "2. ZERA initial review"
  - "3. Public notice/gazetting (14 days)"
  - "4. Technical compliance check"
  - "5. Board approval"
  - "6. License issuance"

## Required Documents
  - "Certificate of Incorporation"
  - "CR6 (List of Directors)"
  - "CR14 (Registered Address)"
  - "Business Plan/Proposal"
  - "Director IDs"
  - "Tax Clearance"
  - "Proof of Application Fee Payment"
  - "Grid Impact Study"
  - "Proof of Land Rights"
  - "PPA (if IPP)"
  - "Financial Capacity Proof"

## Common Bottlenecks
  - "Public objection resolution"
  - "Grid capacity limitations"
  - "PPA negotiation delays"
