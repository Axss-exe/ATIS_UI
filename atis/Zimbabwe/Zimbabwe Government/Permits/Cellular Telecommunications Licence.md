## Cellular Telecommunications Licence

**Country**: Zimbabwe
**Issuing Entity**: [[Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)]]
**Legislation**: Postal and Telecommunications Act [Chapter 12:05]
**Prerequisite Permits**: PTSL Application Requirements
**Processing Time**: 60 to 120 working days


## Description
The **Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)** issues the Cellular Telecommunications Licence under the **Postal and Telecommunications Act [Chapter 12:05]**, granting operators the legal right to provide mobile voice, data, and messaging services across Zimbabwe’s network infrastructure; the licence sets out comprehensive obligations—including spectrum allocation, service quality standards, consumer protection measures, and compliance with national security and emergency communication protocols—while also stipulating fees, renewal procedures, and the conditions under which the licence may be suspended or revoked, thereby ensuring that cellular services are delivered in a regulated, competitive, and consumer‑focused environment.

  - "2. Potraz review"

  - "5. License award"
