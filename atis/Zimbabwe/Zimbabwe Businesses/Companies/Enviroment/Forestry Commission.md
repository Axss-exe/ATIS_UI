# Forestry Commission

## Summary
The Forestry Commission’s mission is “to regulate, conserve and enhance capacity in sustainable management and utilisation of forest resources.”  The Commission, a parastatal under the Ministry of Environment, Climate, Tourism and Hospitality Industry, administers Zimbabwe’s gazetted indigenous forests—covering roughly 0.8 % of the nation’s land—through regulatory oversight, forest‑resource monitoring, and enforcement of the Forest Act (Cap 19:05) and the Communal Land Forest Produce Act (Cap 19:04). Its core operations include afforestation and re‑forestation programmes such as the annual National Tree‑Planting Day, a Seed Centre that supplies certified exotic seed, and the production and supply of seedlings and timber for commercial and community forestry. The agency also delivers research, training, and extension services via forestry colleges and dedicated research units, and it supports wildlife‑based tourism and trade as part of Zimbabwe’s broader conservation and economic strategy. Additional functions encompass the preparation of policy briefs, annual reports, and prospectuses, as well as the management of tenders and partnerships with private and public stakeholders. Governance is provided by a Board of Commissioners, chaired by Ambassador Mahomed Jassat, which offers strategic direction and oversight for the Commission’s sustainable forest‑management agenda.
