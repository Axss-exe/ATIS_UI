# Parks and Wildlife Management Authority (ZimParks)

## Summary
The Zimbabwe Parks and Wildlife Management Authority (ZimParks) states its mission as “to conserve Zimbabwe’s wildlife heritage through protection and sustainable utilisation of natural resources for the benefit of present and future generations.”  ZimParks administers a network of national parks, conservancies and trans‑frontier conservation areas that together safeguard the country’s diverse flora and fauna, including the world’s second‑largest elephant population and significant rhinoceros and bird assemblages. Its operational framework includes wildlife protection, anti‑poaching enforcement, habitat management, and the promotion of sustainable tourism. The Authority employs nineteen terrestrial ecologists and twenty rangers who conduct research, monitoring, and ecological assessments across the protected landscape. Community engagement is pursued through projects such as small‑scale timber production in Nyanga, curio shops in Mutarazi, Matobo, and Tshabalala, and broader conservation‑education programmes that aim to foster coexistence between people and wildlife. ZimParks collaborates with international partners—including the International Fund for Animal Welfare, African Parks, the Frankfurt Zoological Society, and the Global Environment Facility—to enhance capacity, secure funding, and align its activities with global conservation standards.
