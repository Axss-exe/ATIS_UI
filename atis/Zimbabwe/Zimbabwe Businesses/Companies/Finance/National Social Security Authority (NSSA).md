# National Social Security Authority (NSSA)

## Summary
The National Social Security Authority (NSSA) is mandated to administer and manage Zimbabwe’s statutory social security scheme, ensuring the provision of retirement, survivor, disability, and other welfare benefits to eligible contributors and their dependents. NSSA’s core operations include the collection of contributions from employers and employees, the maintenance of a comprehensive contributor database, and the calculation and disbursement of pension and related benefits in accordance with the Social Security Act. The Authority also oversees the registration and accreditation of employers, conducts compliance audits, and enforces penalties for non‑payment or under‑payment of contributions. In addition, NSSA provides actuarial analysis, policy advice, and research to support sustainable fund management, while operating a network of regional offices and service centres that deliver customer support, claim processing, and public education on social security rights and obligations. The Authority collaborates with government ministries, the private sector, and international partners to strengthen institutional capacity, promote financial inclusion, and enhance the overall effectiveness of the social protection system.
