# Zimbabwe Geological Survey

## Summary
The Zimbabwe Geological Survey (ZGS) is mandated to provide scientific, technical, and advisory services for the sustainable exploration, development, and management of the country’s mineral resources, while supporting environmental protection and socio‑economic development.  ZGS conducts systematic geological mapping and mineral resource assessments across the nation, producing geoscientific data and publications that inform policy and investment decisions. It operates a central laboratory equipped for petrographic, geochemical, and mineralogical analyses, offering analytical services to government agencies, academia, and the mining sector. The Survey maintains and updates a national geoscientific database, integrating remote sensing, GIS, and field data to support land‑use planning and hazard mitigation. ZGS collaborates with regional and international bodies on research projects, capacity‑building programmes, and the exchange of best practices. It provides technical training and workshops for professionals and students, fostering expertise in geoscience disciplines. The Survey also advises on regulatory frameworks, contributes to the formulation of mining legislation, and participates in environmental impact assessments to ensure responsible resource exploitation. Through these operations, ZGS aims to enhance the country’s geological knowledge base and promote the effective, sustainable use of its mineral wealth.
