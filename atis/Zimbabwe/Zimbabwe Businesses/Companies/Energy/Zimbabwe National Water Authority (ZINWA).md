# Zimbabwe National Water Authority (ZINWA)

## Summary
The Zimbabwe National Water Authority (ZINWA) mission is “to sustainably provide quality water through strategic infrastructure development and management to facilitate socio‑economic transformation.” ZINWA operates as a state‑owned enterprise under the Ministry of Lands, Agriculture, Fisheries, Water and Rural Development, mandated by the ZINWA Act (Chapter 20:25) and the Water Act (Chapter 20:24) to manage, develop, and sustain the country’s water resources. Its core activities include the administration of water supply from dams to taps, the provision of irrigation water to agricultural users, and the issuance of water‑abstraction agreements that guarantee water allocation based on land‑use requirements. The authority maintains a customer‑service platform that enables online bill payment via mobile money, bank transfers, cash offices, and card transactions, and it processes new‑connection applications for residential, commercial, and agricultural customers. ZINWA also monitors dam levels, publishes water‑herald updates, and oversees ongoing infrastructure projects such as the Semwa Dam and the Tuli‑Manyange Dam. Governance is provided by a board of directors headed by Eng. Bongile Ndiweni, with operational oversight by the Chief Executive Officer, Eng. Taurai Maurikira.
