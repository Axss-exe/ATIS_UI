# Zimbabwe Electricity Transmission and Distribution Company (ZETDC)

## Summary
The mission of the Zimbabwe Electricity Transmission and Distribution Company (ZETDC) is to provide reliable, safe and affordable electricity transmission and distribution services that support the country’s socio‑economic development while adhering to regulatory standards and sustainability principles. ZETDC operates the national high‑voltage transmission grid, managing over 13 000 km of 330 kV, 220 kV and 132 kV lines that interconnect major generation stations with regional distribution utilities. It oversees the planning, construction, operation and maintenance of transmission assets, including substations, transformers and protective equipment, to ensure system stability and minimize outage durations. The company also coordinates with the Zimbabwe Electricity Supply Authority (ZESA) and other stakeholders on load dispatch, grid reinforcement projects and renewable‑energy integration. In the distribution segment, ZETDC administers the allocation of electricity to provincial distributors, monitors power quality, and implements metering, billing and customer‑service frameworks. Additional functions include compliance with the Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA), execution of infrastructure upgrades, capacity‑building programmes for technical staff, and the collection of data for performance reporting and strategic planning.
