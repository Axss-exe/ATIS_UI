# Zimbabwe Power Company (ZPC)

## Summary
ZPC’s mission is to generate and supply electricity sustainably.  Zimbabwe Power Company (ZPC) was incorporated in 1996 as an investment vehicle for electricity generation and became operational in 1999. It is authorised by the Zimbabwe Electricity Regulatory Authority (ZERA) to construct, own, operate and maintain power‑generation stations for the domestic market. The company currently manages five licensed stations with a combined installed capacity of about 1 960 MW: the 538 MW Kariba South hydro plant, the 180 MW Hwange coal‑fired station, the 670 MW Hwange expansion project, and three smaller coal‑fired thermal stations (Bulawayo, Harare and Munyati). Small thermal stations are being phased out; they remain under care and maintenance while repurposing studies are undertaken. ZPC’s generation portfolio supplies roughly 35 % of national output from Kariba, 66 % from Hwange, 28 % from the Hwange expansion, and 59 % from independent power producers (IPPs). The company operates under an Integrated Management System that incorporates ISO 9001:2015, ISO 45001:2018, ISO 14001:2015, ISO 31000:2018, and related standards to address quality, occupational health and safety, environmental stewardship, and risk management.
