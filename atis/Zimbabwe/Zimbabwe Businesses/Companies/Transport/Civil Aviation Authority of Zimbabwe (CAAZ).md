# Civil Aviation Authority of Zimbabwe (CAAZ)

## Summary
The mission of the Civil Aviation Authority of Zimbabwe (CAAZ) is to develop sustainable air transport and offer commercially viable civil aviation safety and security regulatory, air navigation services and related training. CAAZ’s operations are organized into multiple specialised departments that together oversee the nation’s civil‑aviation system. The Flight Operations Inspectorate manages the certification and continuous surveillance of air‑operator certificates (AOC) and foreign operator permits (FOP), approves dangerous‑goods handlers and shippers, and implements fatigue‑risk‑management systems. It also provides technical expertise on the safe transport of dangerous goods, enforces compliance, and processes exemptions and approvals in line with ICAO Technical Instructions and the Zimbabwean Dangerous Goods Regulations (SI 51 of 2018).  The Authority’s broader remit includes aerodrome and inspectorate services, aeronautical information management, air‑traffic services, airworthiness oversight, aviation security, corporate and legal functions, finance, human resources, procurement, public relations, quality‑management, risk management, and internal audit. CAAZ issues licences for personnel, manages NOTAMs, flight plans, and permits, and maintains air‑navigation facilities. It also coordinates training, handles customer enquiries, and publishes safety and security guidance such as prohibited‑item lists and passenger screening procedures. All activities are directed toward promoting safety, regularity, efficiency, and compliance with national legislation and international standards.
