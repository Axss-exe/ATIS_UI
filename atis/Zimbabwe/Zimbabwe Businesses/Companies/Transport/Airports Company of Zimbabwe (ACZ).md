# Airports Company of Zimbabwe (ACZ)

## Summary
The mission of the Airports Company of Zimbabwe (ACZ) is to deliver safe, efficient, and customer‑oriented airport services that support the nation’s air transport needs and contribute to economic development.  ACZ is responsible for the planning, development, operation, and maintenance of Zimbabwe’s principal commercial airports, including Harare International Airport, Victoria Falls Airport, and several regional facilities. Its core activities encompass airside and landside management, ensuring compliance with international safety and security standards, and providing essential ground‑handling, passenger‑terminal, and cargo services. The company also oversees commercial ventures such as retail concessions, advertising, and real‑estate leasing to generate non‑aeronautical revenue. In partnership with airlines, government agencies, and private sector stakeholders, ACZ implements infrastructure upgrades, implements technology solutions for traffic and baggage handling, and conducts regular training programmes for staff. The organization monitors performance metrics, adheres to regulatory requirements, and reports financial and operational results to the Ministry of Transport and Communications, aiming to sustain reliable airport operations and foster growth in Zimbabwe’s aviation sector.
