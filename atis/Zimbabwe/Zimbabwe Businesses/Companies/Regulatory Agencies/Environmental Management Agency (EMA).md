# Environmental Management Agency (EMA)

## Summary
The Environmental Management Agency’s mission is to regulate, monitor and promote the sustainable management of natural resources and protection of the environment with stakeholder participation.The agency carries out a broad range of operations mandated by the Environmental Management Act (Chapter 20:27). It formulates quality standards for air, water, soil, noise, vibration, radiation and waste, and develops guidelines for national, regional and local environmental plans. EMA registers and issues licences and permits, oversees the collection, treatment, disposal and recycling of waste, and monitors emissions of pollutants and hazardous substances. It controls invasive alien species, conducts environmental and social impact assessments, and enforces compliance through written orders and periodic audits of projects, including those initiated before the Act’s implementation date. The agency maintains registers of licences, prepares five‑year state‑of‑the‑environment reports, and advises the government on international conventions and regulatory reforms. Additional functions include managing ecologically fragile ecosystems, creating model by‑laws for local authorities, regulating access to biological and genetic resources, and implementing incentives for environmental protection. EMA also undertakes any other duties directed by the Minister to safeguard Zimbabwe’s environment.
