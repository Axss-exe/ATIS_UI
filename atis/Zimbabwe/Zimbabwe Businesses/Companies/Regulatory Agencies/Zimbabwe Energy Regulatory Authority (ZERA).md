# Zimbabwe Energy Regulatory Authority (ZERA)

## Summary
ZERA regulates the Zimbabwean energy market cost‑effectively, through incentive regulation and in a fair and transparent manner to achieve sustainable energy.The Authority administers the full spectrum of energy services in Zimbabwe, covering electricity, petroleum, LPG and emerging renewable and alternative fuels. It issues licences, monitors compliance, and enforces standards for generators, distributors, independent power producers, fuel retailers and installers. ZERA maintains a public pricing database that lists current rates for petrol (E20), diesel (D50), electricity (first 50 kWh), and LPG, expressed in both USD and ZWG. It publishes annual reports, resource assessments (solar PV, wind, CSP), and sectoral research, and provides application forms, regulatory guidelines, and a service charter for stakeholders. The regulator also runs consumer‑focused programmes such as dispute resolution, energy‑saving tips, and education campaigns, while collaborating with regional partners and international bodies. Its governance framework includes a board, management departments, and an organisational structure that supports strategic outcomes like improved regulatory capacity, cost‑reflective pricing, increased uptake of modern energy, and enhanced stakeholder satisfaction. All activities are guided by core values of transparency, responsiveness, integrity, teamwork, innovation, and accountability.
