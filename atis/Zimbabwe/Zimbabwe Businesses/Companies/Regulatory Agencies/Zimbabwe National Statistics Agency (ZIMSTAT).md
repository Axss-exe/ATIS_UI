# Zimbabwe National Statistics Agency (ZIMSTAT)

## Summary
The Zimbabwe National Statistics Agency (ZIMSTAT) is mandated to generate, compile, and disseminate reliable, timely, and impartial statistical information that underpins evidence‑based decision‑making, policy formulation, and socio‑economic development in Zimbabwe. ZIMSTAT’s core operations encompass the design and execution of large‑scale data collection programmes—including the 2022 Population & Housing Census, annual economic censuses, and thematic surveys on poverty, income, consumption, labour force, agriculture, health, education, gender, trade, migration, and ICT. The agency maintains a suite of digital platforms—such as the National Data Archive (NADA), the Central Data Catalog, and the OpenSDG portal—to provide public access to microdata, thematic reports, and Sustainable Development Goal indicators. It publishes key macro‑economic indicators (inflation, GDP growth, unemployment, poverty rates) and supports statistical capacity building through internal vacancies for roles in finance, systems development, and human resources. ZIMSTAT also collaborates with government ministries and international partners on initiatives like the Social Registry Survey and the ICT Access and Use Survey, while promoting statistical awareness through participation in African and World Statistics Days. All activities are conducted in accordance with approved accounting standards, statistical methodologies, and data confidentiality protocols.
