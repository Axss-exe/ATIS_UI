# Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)

## Summary
The Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ) is mandated to regulate, promote and develop the postal and telecommunications sectors in Zimbabwe, ensuring that services are affordable, reliable, and of a quality that meets national socio‑economic objectives. POTRAZ’s core operations include licensing and registration of service providers, allocation and management of radio‑frequency spectrum, and the formulation and enforcement of technical and consumer‑protection standards. The Authority monitors compliance with service‑level agreements, conducts market research, and publishes periodic performance reports to inform policy makers and the public. It adjudicates disputes between operators and consumers, oversees interconnection arrangements, and implements universal service obligations to extend coverage to underserved areas. In addition, POTRAZ collaborates with international bodies on best practices, contributes to national ICT strategy development, and provides capacity‑building programmes for stakeholders. The regulator also manages the national postal code system, supervises postal service quality, and facilitates the introduction of new technologies such as broadband, mobile money, and satellite communications, thereby supporting the country’s broader digital transformation agenda.
