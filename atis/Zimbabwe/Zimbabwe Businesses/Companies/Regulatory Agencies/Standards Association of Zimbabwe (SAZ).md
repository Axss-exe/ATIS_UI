# Standards Association of Zimbabwe (SAZ)

## Summary
The mission of the Standards Association of Zimbabwe (SAZ) is to develop national standards and promote their use for socio‑economic development. SAZ operates as a non‑government, non‑profit body incorporated under the Zimbabwe Companies Act and governed by a General Council of 56 representatives from government, local authorities, academia, industry and commerce. Its core activities include the development, publication and dissemination of Zimbabwean standards across sectors such as agriculture, construction, mechanical engineering and ICT. The association delivers standards‑based training programmes, offers third‑party conformity assessment services (including certification, testing, calibration, surveillance, inspection, auditing and registration), and maintains laboratory facilities for raw‑material, product and equipment testing. SAZ also serves as the national WTO/TBT enquiry point, providing standards information and facilitating trade compliance. Financially, SAZ derives revenue from certification, testing, calibration, training and sales of publications, supplemented by a grant from the Ministry of Industry and Commerce and a Standards Development Levy collected from designated employers. The organization is a member of ISO, ARSO, SADCSTAN, COMESA and affiliated with IEC, reinforcing its role in both regional and international standardisation networks.
