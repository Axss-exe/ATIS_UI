# Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe (RBZ)

## Summary
The Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe’s mission is to promote monetary and financial stability, foster sustainable economic growth, and protect the purchasing power of the Zimbabwean dollar while supporting inclusive development.The Bank’s operations encompass a wide range of monetary‑policy, regulatory, and market‑infrastructure activities. It publishes key macro‑economic indicators such as monthly and yearly inflation rates (e.g., 0.48 % M‑o‑M and 4.37 % Y‑o‑Y for ZWG inflation in May 2026) and maintains daily interbank exchange rates for major currencies (USD/ZWG ≈ 26.77, GBP/ZWG ≈ 35.88, EUR/ZWG ≈ 30.96). The RBZ manages foreign‑exchange auctions, oversees the national payment system, and operates a real‑time gross settlement (RTGS) platform with defined operating hours. Regulatory functions include bank supervision, licensing, capital‑flows management, and the administration of a fintech regulatory sandbox with guidelines for responsible innovation. The Bank issues digital token pricing (e.g., ZiG 0.1396 USD per mg) and provides term‑deposit facilities denominated in ZiG. It disseminates research and statistical publications (weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annual reviews), maintains credit and collateral registries, and offers consumer‑protection resources. The RBZ also coordinates monetary‑policy committee meetings, publishes post‑meeting statements, and delivers online services such as the Computerised Exports Payments Exchange Control System (CEPECS) and the Bureau de Change Transactions Reporting System.
