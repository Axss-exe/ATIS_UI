# Procurement Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (PRAZ)

## Summary
The Procurement Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (PRAZ) aims to set standards and regulate public procurement and disposal of public assets in a manner that is transparent, fair, honest, cost‑effective and competitive. PRAZ operates a web‑based electronic Government Procurement (eGP) System that links government ministries, departments and agencies with registered suppliers, enabling electronic tendering, bidding and contract award. The platform, launched on 23 October 2023, entered a pilot phase with a target of onboarding all 350 + procuring entities by January 2024; it currently hosts more than 17 000 registered suppliers, 20 000 + product categories and over 10 000 published tenders. Supporting the eGP system is an eGP Support Service that provides guidance on e‑procurement processes, and an e‑Learning System that offers free training modules on procurement, e‑bidding and related topics. PRAZ also conducts monitoring and evaluation of procurement activities, tracks the number of public procuring entities under its regulatory scope, and reports on the composition of procurement management units (PMUs) versus qualified‑reported members. Recent communications highlight courses on public‑asset disposal, system updates, and upcoming SAPPTA training offerings.
