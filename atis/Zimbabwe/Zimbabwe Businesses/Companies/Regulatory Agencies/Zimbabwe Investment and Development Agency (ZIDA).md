# Zimbabwe Investment and Development Agency (ZIDA)

## Summary
ZIDA’s mission is to promote, plan and implement investment‑promotion strategies that facilitate both local and foreign investment, regulate special economic zones, and support public‑private partnerships to drive economic growth in Zimbabwe. ZIDA operates as the government’s one‑stop shop for investors, offering end‑to‑end services from initial market entry through expansion and after‑care.  Its core functions include providing information on investment opportunities, legal requirements and business procedures; coordinating site visits and administrative support; matching investors with local partners; and managing the issuance and monitoring of investment licences (over 1,000 licences reported).  The agency also oversees the establishment and regulation of Special Economic Zones, promotes public‑private partnerships, and delivers after‑care services to ensure ongoing investor satisfaction.  Recent performance data show that ZIDA secured US $1.4 billion in investment commitments in the first quarter of 2024 and recorded a 2.4 % surge in domestic direct investment to US $102.38 million in Q1 2026, reflecting growing confidence among both foreign and domestic investors.  ZIDA’s team emphasizes investor‑centricity, integrity, excellence and transparency, and it publishes annual and performance reports to track progress toward its targets of US $1 billion in planned capital investment and the creation of 9,000 jobs.
