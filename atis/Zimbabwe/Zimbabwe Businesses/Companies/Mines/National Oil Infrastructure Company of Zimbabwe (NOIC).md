# National Oil Infrastructure Company of Zimbabwe (NOIC)

## Summary
The National Oil Infrastructure Company of Zimbabwe (NOIC) exists to develop, operate and maintain a reliable, efficient and sustainable oil‑product supply chain that underpins the country’s energy security and economic growth. NOIC’s core operations centre on the planning, construction, ownership and management of the nation’s strategic oil‑pipeline network, including the 1,500 km East‑West Pipeline that links the port of Beira to inland depots and the Harare‑Bulawayo corridor. The company also oversees bulk storage facilities, fuel terminals and associated loading‑unloading infrastructure at key hubs such as Harare, Bulawayo, Mutare and Victoria Falls. In partnership with international oil firms and local stakeholders, NOIC provides logistics services, product blending, quality control and safety monitoring to ensure uninterrupted delivery of refined petroleum products. Beyond physical assets, NOIC engages in regulatory compliance, environmental stewardship and capacity‑building initiatives, supporting the Ministry of Energy and the broader petroleum sector in policy implementation and market development. The firm’s activities are financed through a mix of government equity, commercial loans and revenue from tolls and service fees, aiming to achieve long‑term financial viability while safeguarding Zimbabwe’s energy needs.
