# Zimbabwe Mining Development Corporation (ZMDC)

## Summary
The Zimbabwe Mining Development Corporation (ZMDC) was created in 1982 to develop the nation’s mineral resources, generate revenue, and support sustainable economic growth for Zimbabwe. ZMDC is a wholly‑government‑owned entity that manages a portfolio of mining subsidiaries and invests in a range of commodities, including gold, platinum‑group metals, chromite, coal, lithium, and diamonds. Its activities contribute significantly to national gross domestic product, foreign‑exchange earnings, and government revenue. All gold produced in the country is sold to Fidelity Gold Refiners, while the Minerals Marketing Corporation of Zimbabwe (MMCZ) assists in marketing other minerals. The Ministry of Mines and Mining Development regulates the sector, issuing mining titles, special grants, and investment licences, and overseeing compliance with taxes such as capital‑gains tax on title transfers and levies on lithium and stone products. The ZIDA Mining Claims Matchmaking Platform connects claim holders with investors, and the ministry provides guidance on licence renewal, ESG standards, and value‑addition initiatives. Together, ZMDC and the ministry coordinate exploration, extraction, beneficiation, and export controls to maximize the contribution of mining to Zimbabwe’s economy.
