# Mining Affairs Board (MAB)

## Summary
The Mining Affairs Board (MAB) is mandated to promote sustainable development of Zimbabwe’s mineral resources, ensure compliance with mining legislation, and safeguard the economic and environmental interests of the nation.  MAB operates as a statutory board under the Ministry of Mines and Mining Development, overseeing the issuance of mining licences, permits and leases for both large‑scale and artisanal operations. It monitors compliance with health, safety and environmental standards, conducts inspections, and enforces corrective actions where breaches occur. The board maintains a centralised database of mining activities, collates production and revenue data, and publishes regular reports to inform policy makers and investors. It also facilitates stakeholder engagement by coordinating with other government entities, local authorities, and industry associations to resolve disputes and promote best practices. In addition, MAB provides technical advisory services to mining companies, assists in the preparation of feasibility studies, and contributes to capacity‑building initiatives for the mining workforce. Through these functions, the board aims to enhance transparency, attract investment, and support the contribution of the mining sector to Zimbabwe’s Vision 2030 of achieving an upper‑middle‑income economy.
