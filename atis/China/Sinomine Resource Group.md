# Sinomine Resource Group

## Summary
Sinomine Resource Group’s mission is to secure and develop high‑quality mineral resources worldwide, delivering integrated mining, processing and technical services that support the global transition to clean energy and advanced industrial applications. The company operates across four primary segments. In lithium, Sinomine conducts hard‑rock mining, ore dressing and smelting, producing battery‑grade lithium hydroxide, carbonate and fluoride that feed lithium‑ion battery manufacturers, including Tesla, and contributing to renewable‑energy supply chains. Its rare‑and‑light‑minerals division focuses on cesium and rubidium, mining pollucite at sites such as Canada’s Tanco mine and Zimbabwe’s Bikita mine, and converting the ore into high‑purity cesium formate and related fine‑chemical products for oil‑service and other high‑technology sectors. The geo‑tech services arm offers geological prospecting, resource evaluation and technical support for overseas solid‑mineral projects, having executed exploration work in more than 20 countries and participating in major ventures such as Zambia’s Chambishi copper mine and the Congo’s Kamoa copper project. Finally, the mining‑property development segment leverages Sinomine’s Belt‑and‑Road network to trade and distribute a range of mineral commodities—including lithium, cesium, chromium, beryllium and tantalum—linking upstream producers with downstream users. The group’s activities span over 40 nations across Asia, Africa, Europe, the Americas and Oceania.
