# [[VersuchsStollen Hagerbach (Hagerbach Test Gallery)]]

## Summary
The mission of VersuchsStollen Hagerbach (Hagerbach Test Gallery) is to deliver a nationally recognised underground research infrastructure that enables innovative, controlled‑environment research, development, testing and training for academia, industry and public‑sector partners. Operating across 5 km of tunnels and 25 000 m² of caverns, the facility offers modular test fields, laboratories, and training rooms for large‑scale prototyping, material testing, fire‑safety drills and event hosting. Core services include full‑scale testing of energy storage, transport and civil‑protection systems; a high‑quality building‑material laboratory; and a dedicated training centre that provides courses such as EFNARC nozzle‑operator examiner certification, VR‑based spraying simulations and specialist seminars. The Underground Future Lab supports research aligned with UN Sustainable Development Goals 7, 9, 11 and 13, promoting clean energy, resilient infrastructure, sustainable cities and climate‑action solutions.  The organization coordinates project development from concept to validation, assists partners in proposal writing, and facilitates collaborations between universities, research institutes and industry. Additional activities comprise gastronomy and venue services for conferences and workshops, while a transparent management structure ensures open access, FAIR data principles and long‑term collaboration across a broad range of scientific and engineering domains.
